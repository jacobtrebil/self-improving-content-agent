# Bad example — brand carousel

What a weak brand-carousel deck looks like, and why each part fails. Contrast
with `good-001.json` / `good-002.json`.

## The deck (don't do this)

- **Cover:** "DOWNLOAD VIBE HEALTH NOW!! 🚀🔥 THE #1 HEALTH APP OF 2026"
- **Content 02:** kicker "Mistake 03", title "You're probably doing this wrong", body "Most people fail at health because they don't try hard enough. Our app fixes that instantly and melts fat fast."
- **CTA:** title "Click the link!!", body "Download now", button "Download Vibe Health →", bg "ai-bg/70-cta.png"

## Why it fails

- **Hypey, not premium.** All-caps, rocket emojis, "#1 app" — this format is the
  *flagship brand voice*: calm, classy, aspirational. The cover should read like
  a brand, not a flash sale.
- **Wrong tone for the content.** "Mistake 03" / "doing this wrong" is the edgy
  alt-account voice. Brand content uses soft labels ("The idea", "Why it works")
  and builds toward the product, not shame.
- **Unsupported medical claim.** "melts fat fast" / "fixes that instantly" — never.
  Hedge ("can support", "may help") and stay honest.
- **Uses a button + AI background.** This format has **no button and no
  `bg`/`bg_prompts`** — the CTA must use `screens` (real product screenshots:
  `plan` and/or `score`) and a short `cta_text`. The screenshots ARE the CTA.
- **No real logo.** The wordmark is rendered from the asset by `build_brand.js`;
  never type "VIBE HEALTH" as plain text in place of it.
- **Highlight overuse.** Brand decks use mint-green pills sparingly — often zero
  on a slide is more elegant than one.

## The fix

Calm serif cover ("Meet the coach that *never logs off*."), soft-labeled content
that leads to the product, and a close that shows the actual app (`screens:
["plan","score"]`) with a quiet `cta_text` — see `good-001.json`.
